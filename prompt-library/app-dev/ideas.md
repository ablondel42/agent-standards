Find the best app ideas for 2026 based on current trends in progressive web apps, AI-assisted products, and lightweight SaaS.

Primary goal:
Identify ideas that a solo developer or very small team could realistically build in 2 to 6 weeks, launch cheaply, and maintain with minimal ongoing effort.

Core filters:
Only keep ideas that are:
- Genuinely useful and commercially viable
- Simple to build as a PWA, an AI app, or a hybrid
- Cheap to host and operate
- Low-maintenance after launch
- Narrow enough for a strong MVP
- Backed by current trends, not generic startup clichés

Strong preference:
Bias toward ideas that can be built with:
- Simple CRUD
- Search and filtering
- Notifications or reminders
- Dashboards or reporting
- File/text input
- Optional AI enrichment such as summarization, extraction, classification, rewriting, recommendations, or decision support

Avoid ideas that require:
- Complex real-time systems
- Heavy custom AI training
- Large datasets or network effects
- Marketplace liquidity
- Social graph mechanics
- Expensive infrastructure
- High moderation burden
- Heavy compliance or legal risk
- Large support burden
- Mobile-native-only capabilities unless clearly justified
- Broad “AI assistant for everyone” positioning without a tight niche

Research instructions:
- Use current high-trust sources and credible trend signals.
- Focus on changes in user behavior, platform capabilities, AI adoption, underserved workflows, and new opportunities created by modern web capabilities.
- Prefer ideas that solve a narrow but painful problem for a clear user group.
- Prefer ideas that can start small and expand later.
- Include both B2C and micro-SaaS/B2B opportunities.
- Exclude generic ideas unless there is a strong niche, timing advantage, and clear differentiation.

Evaluation criteria:
Score each idea from 1 to 5 on:
- User value
- Ease of development
- Cost to run
- Maintenance simplicity
- Monetization potential
- Competitive differentiation

Prioritize ideas where the first version could be built mostly from standard web app patterns plus API-based AI features, without custom ML infrastructure.
Reject any idea whose MVP likely requires more than one full-time developer, complex DevOps, or ongoing manual operations.
